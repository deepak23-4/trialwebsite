What is Gradient property

When we want to use more than one color in the background of a webpage we use Gradient property. We can assign multiple colors while setting up the gradient property as a background.

Types of gradients and their syntax

  1. Linear gradient
  2. Repeating linear gradient
  3. Radial gradient
  4. repeating radial gradient


Linear gradient
Linear Gradient is used to create a background consisting of transition between 2 or more color along a straight line.
We can assign it from right to left, left to right, top to bottom and bottom to top
We can assign the colors using different angles in degrees.
We can set the colors diagonally
    background:linear-gradient(direction,color-stop1,color-stop2,color-stopn....last-color-stop);
    background-image:linear-gradient(direction,color-stop1,color-stop2,color-stopn.....last-color-stop);
    background-image:linear-gradient(red,blue,yellow,green);
    background-image:linear-gradient(to top,red,blue,yellow,green);
    background-image:linear-gradient(to bottom right, pink,green,red,gray);
    background-image:linear-gradient(45deg,black,white,brown,magenta);
    background-image:linear-gradient(to right,red 0%,red 25%,blue 25%,blue 50%, green 50%, green 75%, purple 75%, purple 100%);


Repeating-linear-gradient
repeating-linear-gradient is used to create linear gradient repeatedly
This gradient property is helpful in achieving colors repeatedly
We can assign 2 or more colors in repeated function
  1. background:repeating-linear-gradient(direction,color-stop1,color-stop2,color-stopn....last-color-stop)
  2. background-image:repeating-linear-gradient(direction,color-stop1,color-stop2,color-stopn....last-color-stop)
    3.
  3. background-image:repeating-linear-gradient(black 0%,black 20%,white 20%,white 40%);
  4. background-image:repeating-linear-gradient(to left,pink 0%,pink 10%,white 10%, white 20%);
  5. background-image:repeating-linear-gradient(to bottom,blue 0%,blue 10%,gray 10%,gray 20%);
  6. background-image:repeating-linear-gradient(45deg,red 0%, red 15%,white 15%,white 30%);
  7. background-image:repeating-linear-gradient(45deg,red 0px, red 15px,white 15px,white 30px);
  8. background-image:repeating-linear-gradient(to bottom left,red 0%, red 15%,white 15%,white 30%);



Radial-gradient
radial-gradient is used to create a background image/ background consisting of transition between 2 or more color that radiates from a given origin point. Here the radial-gradient has an origin point unlike the linear gradient which starts from one whole side
The radial point could be in the center of the background image
The radial point could be at either of the side like top left, top center, top right or center left, center center center right. bottom left, bottom center, bottom right
The radial point could also be defined in offset-x-px, offset-y-px, offset-x-%, offset-y-%
Syntax:
background-image:radial-gradient(shape at position,color-stop1,color-stop2,color-stopn,...last-color-stop);
background:radial-gradient(shape at position,color-stop1,color-stop2,color-stopn,...last-color-stop);
Example:
background-image:radial-gradient(circle at center center,red 0%,blue 100%) shape: It indicates shape of gradient i.e., circle or ellipse.
position: It indicates the position of gradient i.e., top left, top center, top right or center left, center center center right. bottom left, bottom center, bottom right
color-stop: color-position
color: color name, hex color value, rgb(0-255,0-255,0-222), rgba(0-255,0-255,0-255,0-1).
position: px, %
  1. background-image: radial-gradient(circle at top center,red 0%,blue 100%);
  2. background-image: radial-gradient(circle at top right,red 0%,blue 100%);
  3. background-image: radial-gradient(circle at top right,red 0%,blue 100%);
  4. background-image:radial-gradient(circle at center left,red 0px,blue 100px);
  5. background-image: radial-gradient(circle at center center,red 0px,blue 100px);
  6. background-image: radial-gradient(circle at center right,red 0px,blue 100px);
  7. background:radial-gradient(ellipse at center center,red 0%,blue 100%);
  8. background:radial-gradient(ellipse at top center,red 0%, blue 100%);
  9. background:radial-gradient(ellipse at bottom left,red 0%, blue 100%);
  10. background:radial-gradient(ellipse at bottom left,red 0px, blue 100px);
  11. background:radial-gradient(ellipse at top right, cyan 0%,green 25%,orange 50%,blue 75%,red 100%);
